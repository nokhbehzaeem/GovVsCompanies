Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2020

This Privacy Policy describes the privacy practices of Seattle Genetics, Inc. and its affiliates (collectively, “Seattle Genetics,” “we,” “us,” or “our”). This Privacy Policy describes the types of personal information and other information we collect from and about you online and offline when you interact with us or use our websites, apps and other services (collectively, the “Services”), and how we use, store, and share the information collected.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect personal information and other information from and about individuals who interact with us and use our Services. We collect certain technical information automatically through your use of our websites, we collect information voluntarily provided to us, and we collect information from third party sources. The information collected depends on the purpose for which it is being collected. We may combine the information we collect from you with information we receive from third party sources.

Information Collected Automatically through our Websites
When you visit our websites or use our Services, we may collect certain technical information automatically, such as your IP address, device information, information about the network you use to access the Services, browser type and version, geolocation information, your activities while using the Services, sites or services previously visited, and content you see or click on. We use this information to understand and analyze trends, administer the Services, learn about user behavior, and gather demographic information about our users as a whole. For more information about how we and our analytics and advertising partners use cookies and other technologies to collect information when you visit our websites, see the Use of Cookies and Other Technologies section below.

Information Provided Voluntarily
We collect information voluntarily provided by individuals when they interact with us (such as in person, by phone, and through the Services). For example, we collect information provided by:

  • Healthcare Professionals who interact with us through a variety of activities, such as meetings, sales calls, scientific exchanges, advisory boards, patient assistance programs, consulting arrangements, market research, exhibits, presentations, conventions, or in our clinical trials
  • Patients or Caregivers who enroll in or inquire about our programs, such as the SeaGen Secure® support program
  • Clinical trial participants
  • Persons who report an adverse event or product complaint
  • Job applicants who inquire about or apply for a job
  • Investors when they contact us or visit our websites
  • Persons who request information about our products and programs
  • Service providers and business partners with whom we have relationships
  • Other individuals who interact with us in the course of our business

Information From Other Sources
Depending on the interaction or potential interaction that we may have with an individual, we can obtain different types of information about you from a variety of sources, including:

  • Healthcare organizations
  • Healthcare professionals
  • Professional associations
  • Business partners
  • Research and development partners
  • Clinical trial partners
  • Contract research organizations
  • Patients
  • Caregivers or guardians
  • Clinical trial/study personnel
  • Data brokers
  • Service Providers

Here are some examples of how and why we might obtain personal information from third party sources:

  • In our clinical trials, site investigators share pseudonymized personal data of clinical trial subjects with us and service providers (e.g., laboratories that process biological samples, vendors that provide trial subject randomization services and trial supplies) to conduct the trials.
  • We buy data to understand attributes about healthcare professionals and patients such as geography, demographics, needs, specialization, affiliation, product usage, and attitudes.
  • We collect personal information from and about patients and caregivers through activities such as advisory boards, market research, and our patient assistance and advocacy programs.
  • We collect personal information about patients from healthcare professionals and caregivers (e.g., through our patient assistance programs).
  • We may obtain personal information about job applicants or others with whom we may do business by performing reference checks or background screenings.

HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use personal information in a variety of ways to conduct our business and pursue our business interests. We use the personal information we collect to:

  • Register and validate users of the Services
  • Communicate with you for marketing and non-commercial purposes
  • Respond to requests and inquiries
  • Process job applications and administer the employment relationship
  • Administer our programs and process enrollments
  • Conduct clinical trials
  • Conduct research and development 
  • Deliver products and services
  • Improve our products and services
  • Provide customer service
  • Provide and gather information about disease states, developments in our therapeutic areas, our products and brands
  • Administer surveys
  • Perform analytics and provide targeted advertising
  • Administer the Services, analyze trends, and gather demographic information about users
  • Gain insight on our products, pipeline, strategic planning, and unmet medical needs
  • Maintain system security
  • Enter into agreements and enforce contractual rights and obligations
  • Comply with laws and fulfill regulatory obligations (e.g., report on adverse events, process product complaints)
  • Protect our rights and the rights of others

USE OF COOKIES AND OTHER TECHNOLOGIES

We and our analytics and advertising providers use “cookies” and other technologies such as GIF files or web beacons. Cookies are identifiers that facilitate the interaction between your computer or device and the Services. Different types of cookies, such as session and persistent cookies, are used for different purposes. We and our analytics and advertising providers may use cookies or other technologies to facilitate site navigation, personalize your experience, perform analytics, serve you relevant ads (subject to certain restrictions described below), and remember your information when you use the Services. Information collected through cookies and other technologies may include your IP address, geolocation information, device information, browsing activity, and search engine referrals. When you access the Services, our advertising and analytics partners may collect precise geolocation information to provide targeted advertising and analytics services. You can manage the collection of location information using your device setting and controls. Read our Cookies Policy to learn more about cookies and how you can change your preferences.
 

INTEREST-BASED ADVERTISING

Third party advertising partners may use cookies and similar technology to collect certain information regarding use of the Services and other websites automatically and may use the information to serve targeted ads for products and services that may be of interest based on your activity. We may limit interest-based advertising to users in certain jurisdictions as we deem necessary to comply with applicable laws. To learn more about interest-based advertising and how you can opt-out of having your web browsing information used for interest based advertising by companies that participate in the Network Advertising (“NAI”) or Digital Advertising Alliance (“DAA”), visit the NAI (US) and DAA Ad Choices (US) websites. Note, however, that not all advertising providers participate in the NAI and DAA. In addition, if you opt-out, you may still see online advertising from us and third parties, but the ads may be less relevant to you. If you delete or block cookies, buy or use a different device, access the Services from a different device, login under a different name, or change web browsers, you will need to opt-out again. Some web browsers may transmit “do not track” signals; at this time, we do not respond to such signals.

SHARING OR DISCLOSURE OF YOUR INFORMATION

We may share or disclose personal information to conduct our business and pursue our business interests (see How We Use Your Information above). For example:

  • Your personal information may be shared among Seattle Genetics, Inc. and its affiliates who assist us in providing our services and conducting our business.
  • We disclose information to healthcare professionals, third parties (vendors, suppliers, contractors and business partners), and government officials (e.g., law enforcement authorities, the courts, regulatory authorities).
  • We disclose personal information to service providers and affiliates to conduct our business, provide products and services, respond to requests that you make, communicate with you, conduct market research and marketing activities, and provide information about products and disease and health related topics of interest.
  • We share pseudonymized personal data from clinical trials with research and development partners and consultants to help in the conduct and planning for our clinical trials and products. We also share clinical trial data with government authorities to meet regulatory requirements.
  • We share personal information provided by job applicants with third party recruiting agencies and platforms that perform services on our behalf.
  • We will disclose information if we reasonably believe we are required to do so by law, regulation or other government authority (such as reporting safety information to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration), or if we believe it is necessary to protect our company, our website visitors, or others.
  • We may transfer your personal information to a successor entity upon a merger, consolidation or other corporate reorganization in which Seattle Genetics participates or to a purchaser of all or substantially all of Seattle Genetics' assets who agrees to use your information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

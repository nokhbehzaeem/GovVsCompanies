CONMED Corporation (“CONMED” “us,” or “we”) values your privacy.  In this Privacy Policy (“Policy”), we describe how we as data controller collect, use, and disclose information that we obtain about visitors to our website www.conmed.com (the “Site”) and the services available through our Site (collectively, the “Services”).

The Information We Collect About You

We may collect information about you directly from you and from third parties, as well as automatically through your use of our Site or Services.

Information We Collect Directly From You. You may browse our Site without registering.  However, to access certain features and view or download certain content, you may be asked to provide your name, email, other contact information, country, and other demographic information.   In addition, if you submit a “Contact Us,” “Complaint” or other form through the Site, we collect information about you and the reason you are submitting the form, including name, email, other contact information, facility, title, and other relevant information. We will indicate to you whenever it is mandatory to provide information; failure to provide mandatory information may mean that we are not able to fulfill your request or answer your complaint. We may also from time to time ask users to participate in surveys; participation is totally optional.

Information We Collect Automatically. We automatically collect information about your use of our Site and Services through cookies, web beacons, and other technologies. We may combine this information with other information we collect about you. Please see the section “Cookies and Other Tracking Mechanisms” below and our Cookies Policy for more information.  For example, we may collect your domain name; your browser type and operating system type, name and version; web pages you view; links you click; your IP address (including location information); the length of time you visit our Site and use our Services; the content you download; and the referring URL, or the webpage that led you to our Site. Information we collect automatically may be linked with the information that we collect directly from you for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

How We Use Your Information

  • To respond to your requests and inquiries, to communicate with you about your use of our Site and Services, and for other customer service purposes.
  • To tailor the content and information that we may send or display to you, to offer location customization, and personalized help and instructions, and to otherwise personalize your experiences while using the Site or our Services.

  • To administer surveys and questionnaires.
  • To comply with applicable legal or contract obligations, including responding to a subpoena or court order, producing evidence in court or another tribunal or conducting internal investigations.
  • To protect our own rights and interests, such as to resolve any disputes, enforce our Terms of Service, or protect our intellectual and other property.

 

When personal information that has been collected is to be used for a purpose not previously identified, we will identify the new purpose and obtain your consent before using the information for that purpose, unless the use is authorized or required by law.

Our Use of Cookies and Other Tracking Mechanisms

We and our third-party service providers use cookies and other tracking mechanisms to track information about your use of our Site and Services. We may combine this information with other personal information we collect from you.

Cookies.  Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device by websites that you visit. Some cookies allow us to make it easier for you to navigate our Site and Services, while others are used to enable a faster log-in process or to allow us to track your activities at our Site and Service. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but if you prefer, you can edit your browser options to block them in the future. The Help portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell you how to control cookies through that browser. Visitors to our Site who disable cookies will be able to browse certain areas of the Site, but some features may not function. [For more information about the types of cookies our Site uses, please review our Cookies Policy.

Clear GIFs, pixel tags and other technologies. Clear GIFs (a.k.a. tracker gifs, beacons, web bugs or pixel tags) are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on your computer’s hard drive, clear GIFs are embedded invisibly on web pages. We may use clear GIFs to, among other things, track the activities of Site visitors and users, help us manage content, and compile statistics about Site usage. We and our third-party service providers also use clear GIFs in HTML emails to our customers, to help us track email response rates, identify when our emails are viewed, and track whether our emails are forwarded.

Third Party Analytics. We use automated devices and applications, such as Sitecore analytics cookie, to evaluate usage of our Site. We also may use other analytic means to evaluate our Services. We use these tools to help us improve our Services, performance and user experiences. These entities may use cookies and other tracking technologies to perform their services.  We do not share your personal information with these third parties.

Do Not Track. Currently, our systems do not recognize browser “do-not-track” requests. You may, however, disable certain tracking as discussed in this section (e.g., by disabling cookies).
